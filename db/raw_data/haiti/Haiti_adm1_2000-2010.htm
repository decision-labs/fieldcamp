Publication Place:  Washington, DC, US

Description

Abstract:  Annual five-year age/sex group estimates for 2000-2010 for subnational areas of Haiti were prepared by the U.S. Census Bureau and linked as attribute data to boundary shapefiles. The estimates are consistent with the midyear national projections from the U.S. Census Bureau International Data Base.

Purpose:  The U.S. Census Bureau is providing annual subnational population five-year age/sex group estimates for 2000 to 2010 in a geographic information system (GIS) for users of the HIV Spatial Data Repository, funded by the U.S. President’s Emergency Plan for AIDS Relief (PEPFAR) program.

Supplemental Information

Subnational Division:  Haiti first-level administrative division: 10 departments (includes the department of Nippes, formed 2006)

Census Data:  Bureau du Recensement Général del la Population et de l'Habitat (FGPH-2003), Résultats définitifs du Recensement de 2003, [CD], issued May 2006.

Shapefile:  "Haiti_adm1_2000-2010.shp" was created by dissolving the "ADM1_NAME" names in "Haiti_adm2_2000-2010.shp."  Base file for "Haiti_adm2_2000-2010.shp": origin unknown. Provided by John Snow, Inc., October 2007. Boundaries (of the communes) are concordant with the 2003 census of Haiti.

National Estimates and Projections:  U.S. Census Bureau, International Programs Center, Population Division, International Data Base (2000-2010), http://www.census.gov/ipc/www/idb/tables.html.

Metadata Documents:  More information on the subnational population and geographic data uploaded on the HIV Spatial Data Repository by the U.S. Census Bureau can be found in the following documents:

(1)  “Subnational Population Estimates Components for the HIV Spatial Data Repository” provides descriptions of the shapefiles, shapefile metadata, and the spreadsheets with the subnational population data uploaded on the Repository, and it includes a data dictionary.

(2)  “Methods for Estimating and Projecting Population by Age and Sex for Subnational Areas of Countries in the HIV Spatial Data Repository” provides a description of the raking models used for producing the subnational age-sex group population data, which are derived from census data and that are consistent with the national midyear projections in the U.S. Census Bureau International Data Base.

Data Quality Information

 

Attribute Accuracy

Attribute Accuracy Report:  The subnational area names in the shapefile attribute fields are modified as necessary for linking the population estimates to them.  Where one or more subnational areas in a shapefile may differ from those reported in a census, typically because the shapefile may be of a vintage that predates the boundary changes as of that census, records are added to the spreadsheet so that populations can be linked to the “older” polygons.  Frequently these are the aggregates of newer areas that fit the older polygons.  The Haiti shapefile represents the departments as of 2006, the year the department of Nippes was formed from 6 communes that were previously part of the department of Grand‘ Anse.

Completeness Report:  10 polygons represent 10 departments, including the new department of Nippes.

Positional Accuracy:  The U.S. Census Bureau reprojected the "undefined" projection in the original shapefile to the GCS_WGS_1984 projection system. The Census Bureau cannot ensure the preciseness of the digital boundary locations.

Entity and Attribute Information

 

Entity Type Label:  Haiti_adm1_2000-2010

Entity and Attribute Overview:  The shapefile consists of the 10 departments of Haiti, including Nippes formed in 2006.  The attribute data consist of annual 5-year age group population estimates for these areas for 2000-2010 for both sexes and for males and females (denoted respectively with a “B,” “M,” and “F” in the age group label headings). The estimates are based on the 2003 census 5-year age/sex group populations for the departments. The populations from the age groups 80-84 years and 85 years and over reported in the census for each sex were combined into the age group 80 years and over. U.S. Board on Geographic Names (BGN) spellings are used for the subnational area names.  For any area that a BGN spelling is not available, the spelling from the national census is used.  Additional attribute data include any official and other recognized codes for the subnational areas as available.  Information on any alternate area names or recent name or boundary changes is provided in the “COMMENT” field.  More detail on the attributes can be found in the document “Subnational Population Estimates Components for the HIV Spatial Data Repository.” The document is available in the HIV Spatial Data Repository and it includes a data dictionary for the attribute data fields.
